Date Difference Calculator — Count Days, Weeks, and Business Days Between Two Dates

Counting the number of days between two dates by hand is tedious and error-prone, especially when the span crosses months of different lengths or includes leap years. This free date difference calculator does the arithmetic instantly: enter a start date and an end date and get the precise difference expressed in years, months, days, total weeks, total hours, and — critically for project planning — total business days (Monday through Friday, excluding weekends).

How Date Arithmetic Works

The calculation starts by determining the absolute number of calendar days between two dates. From there, the tool breaks the span into complete years, complete months, and remaining days using the same borrow-and-carry logic that date libraries use internally. Because months vary from 28 to 31 days and February gains a day in leap years, manual subtraction frequently produces off-by-one errors. The calculator avoids this by working with UTC timestamps and applying standard Gregorian calendar rules to every intermediate step. The result is a precise, unambiguous breakdown you can trust for legal, medical, or business purposes.

Business Days vs. Calendar Days

For anyone tracking project timelines, shipping windows, or contractual deadlines, the distinction between calendar days and business days matters enormously. Calendar days count every day on the calendar, weekends included. Business days count only weekdays — Monday through Friday. A span of 14 calendar days, for example, contains only 10 business days. The calculator walks through the date range day by day, checking whether each falls on a Saturday or Sunday, and produces an accurate weekday count. Note that public holidays are not excluded because holiday calendars vary by country and jurisdiction; if you need to account for holidays, subtract them from the business-day total.

Add or Subtract Days from a Date

Sometimes the question isn't "how many days between two dates?" but rather "what date is 90 days from today?" The Add/Subtract Days mode handles exactly that. Enter a starting date, specify how many days to add or subtract, and the tool returns the resulting date along with the day of the week. This is useful for calculating due dates, contract expirations, delivery windows, or countdown targets. The calculation correctly rolls across month and year boundaries, including leap years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are business days calculated?
Business days include Monday through Friday and exclude Saturday and Sunday. The calculator does not remove public holidays because holiday schedules differ by country, state, and industry. If you need to account for specific holidays, subtract them manually from the total.

Does this calculator handle leap years?
Yes. The underlying date arithmetic follows Gregorian calendar rules, correctly adding February 29 in leap years. Results remain accurate across century boundaries (e.g., 2000 was a leap year, 1900 was not).

Can I calculate a future date by adding days?
Absolutely. Switch to the "Add / Subtract Days" tab, enter your starting date and the number of days, choose "Add" or "Subtract," and the tool will display the resulting date along with its day of the week.

Is my data stored or sent to a server?
No. Every calculation runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript. No dates or results are transmitted, stored, or logged anywhere. The tool works even if you go offline after the page loads.
