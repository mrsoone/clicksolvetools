1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your browser when you visit. They help the site remember information about your visit — such as your preferences or session activity — and can be set by the site itself (first-party) or by third-party services embedded in the page.

localStorage is a browser storage mechanism that keeps data on your device across sessions. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is never automatically transmitted to any server — it stays entirely on your device until you clear it manually.

3. Managing Your Cookies

You can change your cookie preferences at any time using the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer of every page. This lets you grant or revoke consent for analytics and advertising cookies without leaving the site.

You can also delete cookies and clear localStorage directly through your browser settings. Most browsers let you block all cookies, accept only first-party cookies, or remove cookies when you close the browser.

For more information about how Google uses cookies, visit Google's cookie information page.
